# The Decisions That Come Before Scale
### An AI Lifecycle Playbook for Regulated Environments

**By Jerome Davis | Second Order Strategy**

---

Most organizations didn't decide to use AI in regulated environments. It just showed up — embedded in software tools, introduced through analytics initiatives, adopted by teams chasing efficiency. By the time leadership started asking governance questions, the systems were already running.

This playbook exists to address that reality.

---

## What This Is

*The Decisions That Come Before Scale* is a practical AI governance framework designed for regulated organizations — federal agencies, defense contractors, healthcare organizations, financial institutions, and any enterprise where accountability, compliance, and auditability are non-negotiable.

It is not a theoretical exploration of AI ethics. It is not a checklist built for a single audit. It is an operating model that integrates with how complex organizations already function — and that puts governance in place *before* AI systems scale beyond the point where it can be managed cleanly.

---

## Who It's For

- **Program Managers and Project Leads** accountable for AI delivery
- **Executives and Sponsors** accountable for outcomes and risk acceptance
- **Risk, Compliance, Legal, and Security Professionals** protecting the organization
- **Technical Teams** building systems that must operate in real-world regulated contexts

AI governance cannot belong to any one of these groups alone. It exists at their intersection — and this playbook is written for all of them.

---

## The Problem It Solves

Traditional governance models were designed for deterministic systems that behave predictably and remain static once deployed. AI doesn't work that way.

AI systems are shaped by data, influenced by human interaction, and capable of changing behavior over time. Risk doesn't concentrate at a single control point — it accumulates gradually, surfaces unexpectedly, and becomes visible only after deployment.

This creates structural problems:

- Accountability becomes ambiguous
- Approval boundaries blur
- Controls that once provided confidence no longer map to how systems actually behave
- Technical teams move forward because the tools are available
- Risk and compliance teams struggle to assess systems that don't fit existing categories

The absence of a coherent AI lifecycle isn't a documentation gap. It's an operational vulnerability.

## What's Inside

### Executive Orientation
A governance framework overview — scope, applicability, key roles, escalation structure, and how to use the guide across all phases of AI work.

### Section 1 — Methodology Overview
How CPMAI, ISO/IEC 42001, NIST AI RMF, NIST SP 800-53, and DoD CSRMC are harmonized into a single coherent operating model. Lifecycle alignment across Business Understanding, Data Understanding, Data Preparation, Model Development, Model Evaluation, and Operationalization.

### Section 2 — Governance Phase Gates
Decision checkpoints at each lifecycle phase — what evidence is required, what risks must be assessed, and what constitutes approval to proceed.

### Section 3 — Risk and Security Controls
Mapping AI-specific risk to NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 control families. Bias identification and mitigation per NIST SP 1270. Generative AI security per NIST AI 100-1.

### Section 4 — Roles, Accountability, and RACI
A complete RACI matrix across AI Governance Lead, Program Manager, Data Steward, ML/AI Engineer, Risk Officer, Ethics Officer, and Executive Sponsor.

### Section 5 — Continuous Compliance and Monitoring
Post-deployment governance — Continuous Compliance Validation (CCV), material change criteria, incident response, and audit readiness.

### Appendices
Glossary of standardized terminology, document control procedures, and certification readiness checklist for ISO/IEC 42001.
